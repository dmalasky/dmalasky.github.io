Special Populations

Some people think that if they have certain health issues that they can't do a resistance training program. This isn't true, whether it be an injury, a health defect, or something else there is a way to get you in the gym (Although you should still contact your doctor before doing so). If you have one of these common health issues here are some basic guidelines to keep in mind when planning your workout. And if you still don't feel confident making a program feel free to contact one of our trainers.

Take your blood presure before exercise, don’t train if its ≥160/100. Train all major muscle groups and use machines/free weights to supplement cardio. Also be aware of what your medications prohibit.

Hypertension

Emphasize exercises with bone-loading forces (ex. squat), exercises while standing, and functional movements focus on doing cardio with weights and resitance training.

Osteoperosis

Check glucose before training-levels it should be between 100 & 250, intake 15-30 g CHO every 30 min to maintain this. Your emphasis should be weight loss, train all muscle groups.

Diabetes

Start with isometrics and unweighted movements, progress gradually. Do functional exercises to stabilize joints and increase your range of motion.
